OUR CERTIFICATIONS

Quality and trust are the backbone of our business, along with our other core values.

Our core values are the cornerstones of our business, and so we hold ourselves to particularly high standards of quality and trust.

We want you to have the utmost confidence in booking your travel products with Nordic Visitor. For this reason, we take our accreditations, certifications, and professional memberships very seriously.

Founded and headquartered in Iceland, Nordic Visitor is proud to be fully licensed by the Icelandic Tourist Board and to hold membership with the Icelandic Travel Industry Association.

Nordic Visitor is also involved locally in the countries of our satellite offices, Sweden, Scotland, and Germany. As an Affiliate Member of the Scottish Tourism Alliance, we are proud to take part in ensuring high-quality, trustworthy, and authentic experiences for visitors to Scotland.

As a travel agency, we believe in promoting the culture and natural beauty of our destinations. To ensure these are preserved for future generations, Nordic Visitor plays an active role in our local communities through various sustainability efforts and educational programmes.

We are also a member of Travelife, an internationally recognised sustainability certification. You can learn more about this and our future goals by reading our Sustainability Policy.

If quality and trust are the backbone of our business, then our dedicated and enthusiastic staff are its heart. Nordic Visitor values its team members and is honoured to have been named “Company of the Year” by Iceland’s VR labour union in four years. This is based on employee feedback.

Our Story - Nordic Visitor

OUR STORY

Behind every good journey is a story.

Ours started in Iceland in 2002 with founder Ásberg Jónsson and co-founder Pálmi Jónsson. They turned their passion for exploration into Iceland’s first privately owned bicycle rental and tourist information centre in downtown Reykjavik.

Starting out with only a few staff in a tiny storefront, the start-up soon formed valuable business partnerships. This way, it provided guests more and varied ways to explore Iceland. It grew fast to include the neighbouring Nordic countries, then Scotland and the Alps.

We now have over 100 employees in our Iceland, Sweden, Scotland and German offices. And although we parked the bikes long ago, some things haven’t changed since our early days, including our commitment to high-quality, personal services.
